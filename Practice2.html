The Thirty Years War

1618 - 1648

The Thirty Years War was a religious conflict that took place mostly in the Holy Roman Empire. The War began with the Defenestration of Prague in the Spring of 1618, but the true roots of the conflict lay in the dynastic ambitions of the Hapsburg and Bourbon monarchies, among other causes. The effects were devasting for the population. An estimated 8 million people died during the conflict.

The Major Players

Elector Frederick of the Palatinate

  • Also known as "The Winter King"
  • Was the Calvinist King of Bohemia from 1618-1619
  • Defeated at the Battle of White Mountain

Holy Roman Emperor Ferdinand II

  • Was a memeber of the House of Hapsburg, a powerful, incestuous European dynastic house that ruled Central Europe and Spain.
  • Ferdinand was the Archduke of Austria before he was elected Holy Roman Emperor, and he was a fanatical Catholic.
  • Ferdinand was also the King of Bohemia before he was ousted by the Protestant faction, sparking the initial conflicts of the Thirty Years War.

Elector Maximilian of Bavaria

King Phillip III of Spain



The Major Events

The Defenestration of Prague

  • Occurred in 1618
  • A group of Bohemian Protestants tossed two Catholic government officials out of a window.
  • This action was followed by the Protestants inviting the Elector Frederick to be their new king, ousting current king, Ferdinand II.
